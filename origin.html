about me

My fascination with human consciousness guided my undergraduate studies in cognitive science before centering on sleep research during my five years at a sleep & chronobiology research lab.

Sleep is intimately related with nearly every facet of successful waking life functioning, and yet as a society, we value productivity and sleeplessness at the expense of our health and happiness.

During my first semester at the iSchool, I've been focusing on user experience research & design, which I'm supplementing with coursework & self-study in topics related to product management & entrepreneurship. Within the iSchool community, I serve as the Information Management Student Association's Wellness Chair.

After graduation, I aspire to work at the intersection of health and information technologies, ideally with an emphasis on sleep.
